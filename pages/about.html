Introducing The Exegesist

The Exegesist exists to explore sophisticated horror and its message about the human condition, or more accurately, human abberation. Just as this horror sub-genre itself seeks to do, this project aims at bringing darkness to light.

It also seeks to go beyond filmmakers' intentions, to explore what lies beneath the skin philosophically and theologically, to discuss what these artefacts might assume concerning worldview, ideology, morality, longings and so on.

The project was inspired by my cultural exegesis paper, Zombies! They're Us!, which will be published by the University of Toronto's Journal of Religion and Poplular Culture in 2018. The paper analyses the late George A. Romero's zombie and its progeny. First, it explores and unpacks how Romero and others use the zombie in its context to critique human nature. Secondly, the paper contrues this exegesis through Christian theological categories.
